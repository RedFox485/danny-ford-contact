Playbook
Quick reference. Read before you walk in.
What to Look For ▶
  • "Leave a message" or "We'll call you back" signs
  • Paper appointment books or wall calendars
  • Old or no website (check the window, their trucks)
  • Receptionist juggling phone + walk-in customers
  • Handwritten signs (price lists, hours, specials)
  • "Now hiring" = they're stretched thin
The more of these you spot, the more pain points you can reference. Notice them BEFORE you walk in.
Opening Lines ▶

Lead with their problem

"Hey, I work with trades businesses around here helping them stop losing calls when they're out on jobs. Do you guys ever deal with that?"

If you noticed something specific

"I noticed you don't have online booking -- is that something customers ever ask about?"

The portfolio angle

"I'm building my local portfolio and offering some pretty steep discounts to my first few clients. Who handles your tech stuff?"
Ask questions more than you pitch. Let them tell you what's broken.

Objection Handles ▶

"We're too busy right now"

"That's actually the best time -- you're busy enough that missed calls are costing real money. I handle all the setup. You don't have to do anything."

"We already have a website / system"

"When's the last time it was updated? I see a lot of businesses where the site worked fine 5 years ago but now customers expect online booking and mobile."

"How do I know this works?"

"Fair question. I can set up a small pilot -- if you don't see value in the first month, you don't pay for month two. Zero risk."

"I need to talk to my partner"

"Absolutely. Here's my card. Can I follow up Thursday? That way I'm not bugging you, but you've got time to talk it over."

"Sounds expensive"

"What's your average job worth? If you're missing even 2 calls a week, that's way more than what this costs. And I'm the cheapest out there right now because I'm building locally."

"Not interested"

"No worries at all. Mind if I leave a card? Things change and I'm right here in town."
Closing ▶

If they're interested

  • Get name, phone, email
  • Ask: "What's your biggest headache right now?"
  • Propose next step: "I'll put together a quick plan and send it by Friday"
  • Don't push for payment on the spot -- schedule a follow-up

If they're lukewarm

  • Leave the card
  • Ask if you can follow up in a week
  • Note what they seemed most interested in

Always remember

Be genuine. You're building something real, not reading a script. Ask questions more than you pitch. Log the visit before you drive away.
