Sales Playbook
Quick reference before you walk in.
Before You Walk In ▶

Look for these

  • "Leave a message" or "We'll call you back" signs
  • Paper appointment books or wall calendars
  • Old/no website (check the window or truck)
  • Receptionist juggling phone + customers
  • Handwritten price lists or hours signs
  • "Now hiring" sign (they're stretched thin)

Opening lines

"Hey, I work with trades businesses around here helping them stop losing calls when they're out on jobs. Do you guys ever have that problem?"
"I noticed you don't have online booking — is that something your customers ever ask about?"
"I'm building my portfolio locally and offering some steep discounts. Who handles your tech stuff?"

Objection Handles ▶

"We're too busy right now"

"That's actually the best time — you're busy enough that missed calls are costing you money. I handle all the setup, you don't have to do anything."

"We already have a website / system"

"Totally — when's the last time it was updated? I see a lot of businesses where the site worked fine 5 years ago but now customers expect online booking and mobile-friendly pages."

"How do I know this works?"

"Fair question. I can set up a small pilot — if you don't see value in the first month, you don't pay for month two."

"I need to talk to my partner"

"Absolutely. Here's my card. Can I follow up Thursday? That way I'm not bugging you, but you've got time to chat with them."

"Sounds expensive"

"Totally understand. What's your average job worth? If you're missing even 2-3 calls a week, that's way more than what this costs. But I'm also the cheapest option out there right now because I'm building locally."

"I'm not interested"

"No worries at all. Mind if I leave my card? Things change and I'm right here in Longmont."
Closing & Next Steps ▶

If they're interested

  • Get their name, number, and email
  • Ask what their biggest pain point is right now
  • Propose a specific next step: "I'll put together a quick mockup / plan and send it over by Friday"
  • Don't push for payment on the spot — schedule a follow-up

If they're lukewarm

  • Leave the card
  • Ask if you can follow up in a week
  • Note what they seemed most interested in

Always

  • Be genuine. You're building something, not reading a script.
  • Ask questions more than you pitch.
  • Log the visit in the tracker before you drive away.
